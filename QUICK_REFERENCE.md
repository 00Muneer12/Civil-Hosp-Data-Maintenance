## ðŸŸ¡ MODERATE (Monitored Care)
**What it is**: Stable chronic conditions or acute mild complications  
**OPD Volume**: 35% (15,750 patients)  
**Average Wait**: 30-60 minutes  
**Referral**: Sometimes  
**Intervention**: Monitored Outpatient / Admission if unstable  

### Common Conditions
- Type 2 Diabetes (stable)
- Hypertension (controlled)
- Mild Dehydration
- Stable Asthma
- Mild Gastroenteritis with complications
- Malaria (uncomplicated)
- Typhoid Fever
- Dengue Fever (without hemorrhage)

### Typical Medicines
- Metformin (500-1000mg, 2-3x/day)
- Amlodipine (5-10mg, once daily)
- Azithromycin (500mg day 1, then 250mg)
- IV Fluids for rehydration
- Antimalarials (Chloroquine)

## ðŸ”´ CRITICAL (Life-Threatening)
**What it is**: Life-threatening emergencies requiring ICU/ER  
**OPD Volume**: 5% (2,250 patients)  
**Average Wait**: IMMEDIATE / Resuscitation activated  
**Referral**: YES - To ER/ICU  
**Intervention**: Emergency Room / Intensive Care required  

### Common Conditions
- Cardiac Emergency (MI, Arrhythmia, Cardiogenic Shock)
- Stroke (Ischemic/Hemorrhagic)
- Severe Trauma (Motor vehicle accident, Fall, Assault)
- Respiratory Failure (SpO2 <70%)
- Septicemia/Septic Shock
- Status epilepticus
- Acute Poisoning
- Hemorrhagic Shock

### Typical Medicines
- Multiple high-dose medications
- Aspirin + Clopidogrel + Heparin (Cardiac)
- thrombolytics (Stroke)
- Vasopressors (Noradrenaline, Dopamine)
- Oxygen + Ventilation support

### Vital Signs Expected
- BP: <90 systolic (shock) OR >180 systolic with severe symptoms
- Temp: Variable (may be low in sepsis)
- SpO2: <85% on room air
- HR: >120 bpm or <40 bpm
- RR: >30/min or critical
- Altered consciousness
